The origin

1841

In 1841, Lewis Tappan noticed a problem. As America's economy expanded, merchants were doing business with strangers — people they had no history with, no community knowledge of, no way to evaluate. The question every creditor had was the same: can this person be trusted with money?

Tappan's answer was simple. He built a network of local observers who watched merchants and wrote down what they actually saw. Did they pay on time? Did they live beyond their means? Did they honor commitments under pressure? That behavioral observation, written down and shared, became the Mercantile Agency — and eventually the American credit system that hundreds of millions of people participate in today.

They did not invent wealth. They invented proof of behavior. Everything that followed — mortgages, business loans, economic mobility — was built on top of that proof system.

Nigeria in 2024 has the same unsolved problem Tappan saw in 1841. Over 40 million adults are credit-invisible — not because they are undisciplined, but because there is no system watching and recording their financial behavior. Banks reject them not because they can't manage money, but because they can't prove they can.

Nomad is Tappan's insight rebuilt for the individual. Instead of a local reporter watching a merchant, the protocol itself is the witness. Every lock completed. Every relock honored. Every impulse resisted. Written down permanently. Yours to show.

The outcome we are building toward is not a savings product. It is a proof system — one that lets 40 million Nigerians show lenders, landlords, employers, and institutions exactly who they are with money. Built not from wealth, but from behavior.

Part 01

The Lock

Every deposit enters a time-lock immediately. The lock is not punishment. It is the mechanism that separates you from your impulse. Lock durations are calculated as a percentage of your declared monthly income — making this standard universal across all currencies and income levels.

Global Standard

Lock tiers are income-relative, not currency-fixed. ₦50,000 to someone earning ₦100k/month is a different decision than the same amount to someone earning ₦2M/month. The discipline standard is equal. The naira amount is not the measure — the proportion is.

The Auto-Relock Rule: Matured funds not touched within 6 hours re-lock automatically at half the original duration. You receive three notifications before this happens. The system defaults to preservation — you must actively choose to withdraw.
The Closed Loop: Withdrawals return only to your verified linked bank account. No peer transfers. No third-party destinations. Your money enters Nomad from one account and exits to the same account. This is non-negotiable.

Part 02

Early Unlock

Early unlock is permitted. Your money is always yours. But every breach is logged permanently and the cost scales with repetition. The system does not trap you. It records what you actually did.

Situation Fee Record Impact
First early unlock in any 90-day window 3% of amount −10 points. Logged with date.
Second early unlock in same 90-day window 5% of amount −25 points. Protocol Breach triggered. Loan access suspended 60 days.
Third early unlock in same 90-day window 7% of amount −50 points. Breach Flag visible to lender partners for 180 days.
Breach flags do not disappear. After 180 days of clean behavior they are marked resolved — but the record of the breach remains. A lender sees the full history: the breach, the date, the resolution. This is accurate. This is the point.

Part 05

Failure States

A failure state is not just a point deduction. It changes what the system shows about you to lenders and partners. Failure is permitted — it is recorded accurately.

Level 1

Single Breach

  • First early unlock in 90-day window
  • −10 points deducted
  • Logged internally with date and amount
  • Recoverable within same period

Level 2

Pattern Breach

  • Second early unlock in same 90-day window
  • −25 points deducted
  • Protocol Breach flag triggered
  • Loan access preview suspended 60 days regardless of total score

Level 3

Chronic Breach

  • Third early unlock in same 90-day window
  • −50 points deducted
  • Breach Flag visible to all lender partners
  • Flag active for 180 days of clean behavior
  • Cannot advance conduct levels during flag period

Level 4

Dormancy

  • Account inactive 90+ days with locked funds
  • 3 notifications sent over 7-day window
  • No response triggers safety auto-relock
  • Dormancy period noted on record
Breach flags resolve, they do not disappear. After the 180-day clean period, the flag is marked resolved. The original breach date, amount, and context remain permanently on file. A lender reading your record in year three sees year one clearly. This is not punitive — it is accurate.
