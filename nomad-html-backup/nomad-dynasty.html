Without Dynasty

Parents save in accounts they can still access under social or financial pressure. The money gets raided for emergencies — real and imagined. By the time the child reaches adulthood, there is nothing. The cycle restarts from zero.

With Dynasty Lock

Once irrevocable mode is set, the parent's early unlock is permanently disabled. The protocol protects the beneficiary from the grantor's future pressure. The child reaches their sovereignty age with capital intact — and a handover structure that prevents immediate consumption.

How it works

Sovereignty transfer
by protocol.

01

Initialize the Fortress

Grantor creates a Dynasty Lock. Sets the beneficiary — name and date of birth. Chooses the Age of Sovereignty: 18, 21, 25, or 30. Monthly deposit protocol starts immediately.

02

Set Irrevocable Mode

After a consistency threshold is met, the grantor can activate Irrevocable mode. From this point, no parent-side early unlock is possible. The protocol itself holds the line — not willpower.

03

The Lock Runs

Monthly deposits continue building. The balance is visible on the dashboard but untouchable by the grantor once irrevocable. Nomad Insight tracks growth and Phase 2 deployment readiness.

04

The Handover

30 days before the target age, the transfer-readiness window opens. Beneficiary completes identity verification. Vault ownership migrates. Nomad Insight guides deployment — preventing the inheritance consumption pattern.
